# Code of Conduct

kotoji is a small, maintainer-led open-source project. The rule is simple:
**be respectful and act in good faith.** Treat everyone — the maintainer, other
contributors, and users — the way you'd want to be treated.

## Expected

- Be kind, and assume good intent.
- Respect differing opinions, experience levels, and backgrounds.
- Give and accept constructive feedback gracefully.
- Keep discussion focused on the project.

## Not OK

- Harassment, insults, or personal/political attacks.
- Sexualized language or imagery, or unwanted attention.
- Publishing someone's private information without their permission.
- Anything you wouldn't say or do in a professional setting.

## Scope

This applies in all project spaces — issues, pull requests, discussions — and
anywhere you are representing the project.

## Reporting & enforcement

If someone's behavior is a problem, let the maintainer know through GitHub:

- For anything **sensitive or private**, use the repository's **Security →
  Report a vulnerability** form. It is the project's private channel to the
  maintainer — just note that your report is about conduct, not a security bug.
- For everything else, open a regular issue.

The maintainer reviews reports in good faith and may edit, remove, or reject
contributions — and warn or block people — at their discretion to keep the
project welcoming. Reporters' privacy is respected.

---

This is a deliberately lightweight Code of Conduct for a solo-maintained
project. It is inspired by, but simpler than, the
[Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org).
